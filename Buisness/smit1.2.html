Earth

Fun Facts About Earth

  • Earth is the only planet known to have **liquid water** on its surface.
  • It is the **fifth largest planet** in our solar system.
  • Earth's atmosphere is composed mainly of **nitrogen (about 78%)** and **oxygen (about 21%)**.

Basic Information

Earth's atmosphere protects us from harmful radiation from the Sun and helps regulate the planet's temperature, making life possible.

Our **oceans cover about 71%** of the Earth's surface and play a crucial role in global climate and weather patterns.

The land on Earth is constantly changing due to **tectonic plate movement, erosion, and volcanic activity**, shaping the continents and landscapes we see today.

Solar System

Interactive diagram of the solar system will be implemented here.

Our solar system consists of the **Sun** and everything that orbits it, including eight planets, numerous dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, and comets.

The planets in our solar system, in order from the Sun, are **Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune**.

The Sun, a massive **star** at the center of our solar system, provides the immense light and heat that are essential for life on Earth and drives most solar system phenomena.

Basic Physics of Space

Gravity and Orbits

Gravity is the fundamental force that pulls all objects with mass towards each other. It's the invisible hand that keeps the planets in stable orbits around the Sun and moons gracefully circling their planets.

Interactive simulations of planetary motion and gravitational interactions will be added here.

Understanding gravity is key to comprehending the vast scale and intricate dance of celestial bodies in our universe.
